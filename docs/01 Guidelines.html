01 The Idea

A budget made visible.

Most money apps look like dashboards. PULDAR looks like a doctrine. It takes a 100-year-old idea — split your income into three buckets and live inside the split — and turns it into a brand, a product, and a discipline.

The principles

i.Three buckets. No others.

Fundamentals (50%), Fun (30%), Future (20%). Every dollar lives in one of three places. The product never invents a fourth.

ii.Discipline is the feature.

PULDAR doesn't gamify saving. It removes friction from the right choice and exposes the cost of the wrong one. The brand should feel calm, slightly stern, and on your side.

iii.Less, but better.

Dieter Rams > Duolingo. We remove rather than add. One graph. One tap. One number. The mark itself shows what we kept.

The mark, in one line

Three bars sized to a 50 / 30 / 20 split. The math is the logo. The space above the bars — the headroom — is what you keep.

02 The Mark · Construction

The mark is drawn on a 100-unit square. Three bars of equal width sit on a shared baseline. The heights are not decorative — they encode the default budget split.

Bar x y w h Radius Fill
Fundamentals (50%) 141522703#3A5CAD
Fun (30%) 424322423#2A8056
Future (20%) 705722283#C96924
Bars are bottom-aligned. Inner padding is 14 units left, 8 right. Corner radius is 3 — present, not soft.
02·b The Mark · Clear Space & Minimum Size

Clear space

Reserve a margin equal to 1 bar-width (one X) around the mark. Nothing — no type, no edge, no other graphic — sits inside this zone.

Minimum size

The mark holds together down to 16 px on screen and 8 mm in print. Below that, switch to the monogram or fall back to the wordmark alone.

04 Do & Don't
Do Use the mark in its given form, on cream or white.
Don't Don't stretch, squash, or change the aspect ratio.
Don't Don't recolour the bars individually. The trinity palette is the only palette.
Do Use the cream monogram on dark grounds where colour reproduction is unsafe.
Don't Don't outline the bars. The mark is a solid form, not a frame.
Don't Don't rotate. The mark always sits upright on its baseline.

06 Typography

One typeface, set with intent. Helvetica Neue carries every word in the brand — from billboards down to the row labels in the app. Tight letter-spacing on display sizes, loose tracking on caps eyebrows.

07 Voice

PULDAR sounds like a calm accountant who likes you.

Direct, plain, slightly dry. Never cheerful, never finger-wagging. We don't explain the obvious. We don't add exclamation marks. We let the numbers do the threatening.

We sound like this

✓"You spent £312 on Fun. The budget said £400. Good."
✓"Rent is due in 6 days. Fundamentals can cover it."
✓"You've kept 14% this month. Move it to Future?"
✓"Less, but better."

Not like this

✗"🎉 Great job! You crushed your budget this month!"
✗"Uh oh — you're spending a bit too much on Fun. Let's reel it in!"
✗"Your financial wellness journey continues…"
✗"Hey friend, just a little nudge…"
